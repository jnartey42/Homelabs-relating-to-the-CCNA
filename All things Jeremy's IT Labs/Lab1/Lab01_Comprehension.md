Comprehension: This video is an introduction to what networking entails. How to understand basic network topology and vocabulary, and what goes into a network, beginning with terms such as routers, switches, end hosts, servers, clients, the internet, firewalls, and so on. The aforementioned are all network nodes -- different devices, when working together, are allowed to share resources with each other. Routers share data between different networks over the internet, switches share data inside networks, end hosts can refer to clients, such as a computer sending or receiving data, servers hold resources that are readily available to be requested by clients, the internet helps transmit data, and firewalls help filter out harmful traffic going in and out of networks. 

Lab: Jeremy's lab encouraged familiarity of Cisco's Packet Tracer program, as well as how to build a network. Utilizing the switches, routers, firewalls, PCs, available, I created two networks, with each one having a switch, router, firewall, and the NY Branch network having two PCs, whilst the Tokyo Branch network has two servers. A laptop at the bottom is there to represent a potential malicious attacker, trying to breach a network. The firewalls will most likely filter out unfamiliar traffic. All the devices are connected together with "Auto Choose Connection Type".
